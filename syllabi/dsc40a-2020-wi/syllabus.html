You can register your iClicker using the link on this course's Canvas page (we won't use Canvas for anything else).

Message Board

We'll use Campuswire as the course message board. I'll post all announcements there, so make sure you have access. You can also ask (and answer) questions about course topics and message other people in the class.

Tokens

Many courses have separate, strict policies for attendance and late homeworks. Instead, DSC 40A is flexible: it allows you to build your own policy.

At the beginning of the quarter, you are given two tokens. At the end of the quarter, these tokens can be used to turn late homeworks into on-time homeworks, and to add points to your homework score. Here's the twist: additional tokens can be earned throughout the quarter by attending lecture.

Here's how it works: for every four lectures you attend, you earn one extra token (attendance is taken using the clicker discussion questions). There are 18 total lectures, so if you attend every lecture you'll get 4 extra tokens. Combined with the 2 you get at the beginning of the quarter, you'll end up with 6 tokens.

At the very end of the quarter, I'll check to see which of your homeworks were submitted within the late period (24 hours after the due date). Each token you have will be automatically used to flip one "late" homework to "on-time".

If you have tokens left after this, they'll automatically go towards boosting your homework score. Here's the conversion rate:

1st leftover token +50 points
2nd leftover token +25 points
3rd leftover token +15 points
4th leftover token +10 points
5th leftover token +10 points
6th leftover token +5 points
These points are added to the total number of points you earned over all homeworks (there will be around 800 points available), up to a maximum of full credit (you can't get more than a 100% on your homework).

You don't have to do anything to use your tokens — I'll apply them automatically at the end of the quarter.

Example 1: the person who skips every class.

Zelda attends only one lecture — the first one — and turns in one homework late. At the end of the quarter she'll have two tokens. One will go towards negating her late homework, the other will add 50 points to her homework total.

Example 2: the person who is always late.

Winona attends every class, but turns in all of her homeworks late. She gets six tokens for attendance, and six of her late homeworks are considered on-time, but she has no points added to her homework total. Moreover, one of her homeworks receives a zero, because it was late and she didn't have enough tokens to mark it as on-time.

Grading Scheme

Your final grade will be calculated using the following breakdown:

  • 25% Homework
  • 30% maximum of Midterm 01 and Midterm 01 Redemption
  • 30% maximum of Midterm 02 and Midterm 02 Redemption
  • 15% Final Exam

Midterm 01 will cover lectures 1—7, Midterm 02 will cover lectures 8—14, and the Final Exam will cover lectures 15—18. However, the final exam will also have two, optional redemption sections which will allow you to completely replace your midterm scores. Each redemption section will be similar in length to the midterm it is replacing, but there's no guarantee that it will be the same level of difficulty.

Letter grades will be assigned using the traditional scale (e.g., a 93% and above is an A, a 90% to a 93% is an A-, etc.). Minor adjustments to the scale may be made only after all grades are computed; these will never result in your grade decreasing.

All of your grades will be available on Gradescope. We won't use Canvas.

Regrades

You can ask for a regrade on any assignment if you believe that the grader made a mistake. Remember that clarity is a part of your score — if you had the right idea but were unable to clearly communicate it, you may still not deserve full credit. We ask that you please submit your regrade requests within one week of the assignment being released.
